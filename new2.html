You or someone you're working with might want the website for a variety of reasons. Websites can inform the public, encourage communication, offer promotions or provide business services, such as e-commerce or appointment scheduling. Consider why you want to create a website and what its purpose will be.

Creating a new website is a different process than redesigning an existing one. If you're updating a website, there are other questions that you may ask yourself to help you create a better site. Some questions you can ask if you're redesigning a website include why you're redesigning the page, what you like about the old website and what features need to change.

When designing a website, you may want to ask yourself what technical specifications your site may need to perform properly. For example, if the website has graphics, music and multiple pages with various features, it may require more bandwidth than a site that only contains a background and text. Try to think about your technical capacity in advance to ensure that your website runs smoothly.

Try to plan the main function and scope of your project before the creation process. Think about your desired website size and the main functions that you need. Planning the range of services your website provides in advance helps you focus while making the site and may reduce the overall price if you're using external assets or contractors.

The amount of money you have to spend on a website can influence the design process. A large budget can help you create a complicated website with multiple features and help from outside contractors, while a smaller budget may require fewer functions and limited staff. When designing a website, try to determine how much money you can spend on the project and the approximate cost of the features you need.

While you may want several different functions or features for your website, deciding which ones are necessary can help you prioritize your work and help you make decisions if you're limited by budget or if you encounter any challenges. Consider brainstorming a brief list of tasks that you require your website to perform. You can use your core values and the website's specific purpose to help you create your list.
